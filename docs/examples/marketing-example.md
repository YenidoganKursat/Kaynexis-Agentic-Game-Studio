# Marketing Example

## Scope

Steam-first indie survival game.
Goal: grow wishlists before the first public demo while keeping the strategy honest about current market signals.

If the user wants current marketing status instead of strategy, switch to `docs/reference/marketing-intel.md` and `docs/examples/marketing-intel-example.md`.

## Baseline

- Steam page exists but conversion is not yet measured.
- One trailer and a small screenshot set are available.
- Community presence exists, but the feedback loop is informal.
- No paid acquisition plan has been validated.

## Decision order

1. Pick the objective.
2. Name the audience.
3. Choose the best channel family.
4. Confirm the asset proof points.
5. Define the measurement path.
6. Decide what should wait.

## Example strategy

| Objective | Audience | Channel | Required assets | Success metric |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| Wishlist growth | PC survival players who like readable pressure and crafting | Steam page plus one community beat | trailer, screenshots, capsule art, CTA copy | page visit to wishlist conversion |
| Demo installs | Players who need to feel the loop before they trust it | demo beat or festival event | demo build, short feature list, honest known-issues note | installs, retention, return visits |
| Creator reach | Players who discover games through clips or live play | targeted creator outreach | clip-friendly gameplay, one-line hook, capture-ready build | referral traffic, mentions, wishlist adds |
| Retention and trust | Existing followers and early players | community updates and patch beats | patch notes, screenshots, response plan | reply rate, sentiment, returning users |

## Good agent prompts

- "Decide whether our next marketing move should be a Steam page push, a demo beat, or creator outreach, and justify it with source-backed criteria."
- "Compare the current trailer, screenshots, and message house against the audience we want to reach."
- "Build a measurement plan for a wishlists-first campaign and show which metric proves the channel is working."

## Validation

- The plan names the objective, audience, channel family, and first metric.
- The plan cites the official source family used for the current window.
- The plan separates facts from inference and states one repo implication.
